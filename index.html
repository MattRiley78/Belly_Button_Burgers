Would You Eat a
Belly Button Burger?
Use the interactive charts to explore the dataset!

Believe it or not, those innovative, tastes-just-like-beef vegan burgers are not just a combination of black beans and beet juice. A large initiative is underway to synthesize edibile proteins from micro-organisms, especially ones that taste like our favorite foods. Studies have already been performed that have synthesized proteins from microbes found in Yellowstone National Park. Some of these impossible patties may have already shown up at your trendy burger shop.

Where will be the next big source for protein synthesized from microbes? Some believe we have to look no further than our own navels. That's right, the good old belly button. It fed us while we were in the womb, and it can feed us again. There is a plethora of bacteria and other microbes found in that funny knob above our waists.

This page is the result of a collection study of the great biodiversity found in belly buttons. These charts provide anonymous information about our study participants. Do the types of bacteria differ for different ages, genders, locations, ethnicities? Is all that good microscopic food source washed down the drain from basic cleanliness?
